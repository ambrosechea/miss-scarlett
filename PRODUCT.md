# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Brides-to-be exploring gowns and boutique stockists evaluating whether to carry the label. Brides arrive aspirational and emotionally invested — they want to feel seen, inspired, and confident in their choice. Stockists arrive commercially — they need to trust the brand's positioning, quality, and market fit. Both share the moment of discovering something that feels right.

## Product Purpose

Miss Scarlett is a luxury bridal label. The site exists to translate the physical experience of the gowns — the weight of the fabric, the precision of the cut, the feeling of putting on something made with intention — into a digital presence that earns the same response. Success is a bride finding her stockist and walking into a fitting already in love with the brand.

## Brand Personality

Refined, modern, graceful. Understated luxury with feminine confidence. Not precious or fragile — composed. The kind of quiet authority that comes from knowing every detail was considered. Speaks with restraint but never with apology.

## Anti-references

- **Over-the-top glam:** Heavy ornamentation, glitzy gold, maximalist embellishment. Miss Scarlett's luxury is sculptural, not decorative.
- **Generic template bridal:** Stock-photo-heavy, cookie-cutter layouts, predictable pastel palettes, interchangeable with any other bridal brand. The site should feel authored, not assembled.
- **Fast-fashion / discount:** Cluttered layouts, sale banners, urgency tactics, anything that undercuts the luxury positioning. Price is not the conversation.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Let the gowns and imagery carry the emotional weight. Copy supports; it never substitutes.
2. **Earned confidence, not decoration.** Every visual choice — spacing, weight, scale — should feel deliberate. Ornament only where it serves the garment's story.
3. **Every detail is intentional.** From button radius to section rhythm, nothing is default. The site should feel as considered as the gowns themselves.
4. **Breathe.** Generous whitespace and pacing let the imagery land. Crowding undermines luxury.
5. **One voice, two audiences.** The same design must inspire a bride and convince a buyer. Lead with emotion; the commercial credibility follows from the craft.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the practical baseline: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, keyboard navigability, meaningful alt text on all imagery. Reduced-motion alternatives for any animation. No formal compliance target, but the luxury positioning demands that the experience feels considered for everyone — accessibility is part of the craft, not a checkbox.
